# Douyin and WeChat Channels Launch

## Brief before script

For every video define:

1. Target viewer and situation;
2. Reason to watch now;
3. Account identity or recurring promise;
4. One idea, task, demonstration, conflict, or decision;
5. Evidence visible on screen;
6. Spoken and visual structure;
7. Permitted next action;
8. Risk and claim review.

The opening must identify relevance quickly, but do not enforce a universal three-second formula. Match pacing and length to the topic, format, audience, and actual retention evidence.

## Useful formats

Demonstration, field note, failure analysis, before/after with method, comparison with criteria, public experiment, customer question, behind-the-scenes process, checklist, and recurring challenge can all work when truthful and native to the account.

## Platform distinction

- Douyin: test native visual and narrative formats, search phrasing, series logic, commerce fit, and audience quality using current platform capabilities.
- WeChat Channels: evaluate video performance together with legitimate WeChat ecosystem paths, audience relationships, live or commerce operations, and service capacity. Do not assume social interaction has a fixed algorithmic weight.

## Review

Analyze exposure eligibility, opening retention, completion, rewatch where available, comments by motive, sharing context, profile visits, follows, qualified messages, commerce actions, and operational outcomes. Use internal medians and comparable formats; fixed universal thresholds are unreliable.

Do not ask private groups or friends for coordinated likes, comments, or shares. Ask for private quality feedback or share content only when it is genuinely relevant, without directing platform behavior.
