# X/Twitter Cold Start

## Positioning

Define three related themes, one target reader, one recurring problem, a follow reason, and a boundary. Make the profile, pinned post, recent posts, and linked assets support the same promise.

## Content system

Build from real work:

- tool or process tests;
- mistakes and changed decisions;
- cases with context and limitations;
- templates and checklists;
- concise judgments supported by experience;
- replies expanded into posts or threads;
- posts consolidated into durable documents or skills.

Treat replies as standalone value. Contribute a concrete experience, example, distinction, or question; do not use generic praise as a visibility tactic.

## Review

Separate impressions, useful interaction, profile visits, follows, qualified conversations, link actions, and reusable assets. A reply with reach but no relevance may not support the account promise. Review the kinds of people and questions attracted, not just totals.

Do not automate mass replies, scrape or message people without authorization, manufacture controversy, join engagement rings, or chase unrelated trends. Verify current X rules, API limits, automation, paid features, and disclosure requirements before operational advice.
