# Editorial QA

## Reader test

- Is the intended reader and situation clear early?
- Does the opening make a specific promise?
- Does every section add evidence, reasoning, action, example, or boundary?
- Can the reader use the content without contacting the author?
- Does the CTA follow naturally from the unresolved context?

## Trust test

- Attribute quotations, numbers, and external claims.
- Distinguish a company claim from independent evidence.
- State sample, time, market, and limitations where relevant.
- Remove guarantees, universal claims, unverifiable superlatives, and invented precision.
- Disclose sponsorship, commercial relationships, and synthetic media when required.

## Language test

Remove generic openings, stacked slogans, repeated summaries, fake quotations, excessive rhetorical questions, and mechanical transitions. Prefer concrete nouns, active verbs, short paragraphs, and natural variation in sentence length.
