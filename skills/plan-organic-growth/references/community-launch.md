# Community and launch execution

## Reddit research

- Find communities through user problems, categories, competitors, and identities.
- Read rules, pinned posts, FAQs, moderator behavior, and past removals.
- Collect recurring questions, exact language, workarounds, objections, purchase triggers, and unmet needs.
- Validate signals across time, users, and communities before converting them into product or content decisions.

## Reddit participation

- Use a truthful founder, expert, employee, or brand identity.
- Make each contribution useful without requiring a link.
- Disclose commercial relationships and answer criticism directly.
- Respect subreddit-specific rules and moderator decisions.
- Avoid copied posts, vote coordination, bought karma, sockpuppets, fake reviews, and ban evasion.

## Product Hunt and cold start

- Prepare positioning, demo, visuals, pricing, FAQ, analytics, support, and failure handling.
- Build genuine relationships before launch; do not purchase votes.
- On launch day, respond, classify feedback, fix critical issues, and follow up with qualified users.
- Evaluate visits, activation, payment, retention, feedback quality, and reusable learning—not ranking alone.

For first users, combine direct founder outreach, relevant communities, search-intent pages, useful tools/templates, partners, cases, and referrals.
