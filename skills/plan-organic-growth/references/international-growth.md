# International Growth Planning

## Channel selection

Choose channels from demand behavior, product demonstration, buying process, unit economics, localization needs, and team capacity. Search fits active demand; visual social fits demonstrable products; professional networks fit complex B2B trust; communities fit research and participation; product-led loops fit collaborative or output-generating products.

## Market-entry sequence

1. Validate the customer problem and willingness to act;
2. Localize value proposition, proof, offer, support, legal terms, payments, and measurement;
3. Find early users through direct research and small channel tests;
4. Build repeatable content, search, community, partner, creator, and product loops;
5. Add paid acquisition only when conversion, retention, and unit economics are measurable.

Avoid universal price bands, click-through thresholds, daily creative quotas, fixed scaling percentages, follower milestones, and claims that a platform or format always works. Verify current commerce eligibility, disclosure, privacy, payment, tax, and advertising rules in the target market.
