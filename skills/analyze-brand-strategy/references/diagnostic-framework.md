# Brand Diagnostic Framework

## Evidence ledger

Tag every important claim as company statement, customer evidence, behavioral data, independent source, observation, or inference. Add date, geography, sample, and confidence.

## Nine diagnostic dimensions

1. Customer job, tension, and usage occasion;
2. Category frame and substitute set;
3. Functional, emotional, social, and economic value;
4. Product, service, and price architecture;
5. Distinctive assets and memory structure;
6. Distribution, availability, and channel trust;
7. Experience, operations, and repeat behavior;
8. Culture, community, and reputation;
9. Organizational capabilities and measurement.

## Option comparison

Score each strategic option on customer relevance, distinctiveness, credibility, economic value, operational feasibility, channel fit, reversibility, and time to evidence. Explain trade-offs; do not hide them inside a total score.
