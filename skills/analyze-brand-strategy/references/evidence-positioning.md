# Evidence-aware positioning

Use this reference when the task requires positioning, differentiation, repositioning, category choice, or a one-line value proposition.

## 1. Readiness gate

Start research only when all six items are available:

1. Identifiable subject and business type;
2. Concrete decision the positioning must support;
3. Offer described in terms of what the customer receives;
4. Market boundary with geography and language;
5. Initial target-user or purchase-trigger hypothesis;
6. At least one inspectable first-party input, attachment, interview record, or official URL.

If the gate fails, ask at most three blocking questions. Do not generate a confident position from a business name and a feature list.

## 2. Evidence labels

Label important claims as fact, company statement, customer evidence, behavioral evidence, observation, inference, hypothesis, or recommendation. Record date, geography, sample, source perspective, market fit, confidence, conflicts, and falsification condition.

An official page proves what a company currently claims. It does not alone prove customer perception, market leadership, or outcome. A search result or AI summary is a discovery lead, not final evidence.

## 3. Demand record

For every material demand, record:

- customer segment and choice situation;
- desired job or result;
- event that makes the issue urgent;
- current solution and cost of inaction;
- decision criteria;
- willingness signal such as budget, trial, migration, time, or purchase behavior;
- evidence and confidence.

An opportunity requires important demand, insufficient supply on a key criterion, and a credible ability to deliver. An empty phrase or missing competitor is not an opportunity by itself.

## 4. Five competition roles

Cover:

1. Direct competitor: same category, user, and situation;
2. Indirect alternative: different method for the same job;
3. Current workaround: manual process, internal team, incumbent supplier, or habit;
4. Inaction: delay, tolerate, or do nothing;
5. Mental benchmark: the reference the buyer already trusts or recalls.

For each, capture target user, category, promise, keywords, offer, price status, channel, proof, user-side choice reasons, weakness, and evidence boundary. Preserve conflicts between official claims and user experience.

## 5. D6 advantage test

Test every candidate advantage on:

- customer relevance;
- competitive scarcity;
- evidence strength;
- defensibility;
- strategic fit;
- expression focus.

Reject it as a core position when the user does not care, evidence is weak, competitors widely provide it without a different mechanism, or it cannot map to a concrete choice situation.

## 6. Positioning six questions

Answer before recommending:

1. What position does the subject occupy now?
2. What single, understandable position should it occupy?
3. Which competitor, workaround, or old belief must it replace?
4. Are resources sufficient to enter and defend it?
5. Can the organization sustain the focus?
6. Do product, price, content, channel, sales, and experience reinforce it?

## 7. Option design and recommendation

Create two or three options that differ materially in target user, choice situation, category, mechanism, alternative, or delivery. Do not treat different slogans as different strategies.

Compare customer value, competitive distinctiveness, category clarity, proof, operational feasibility, channel fit, education cost, risk, reversibility, and time to evidence. Recommend one and state sacrifices, risks, and falsification conditions.

Use one of three states:

- `execute`: demand, competition, and capability evidence support the recommendation;
- `validate_first`: direction is plausible but one important evidence link is weak;
- `insufficient_evidence`: the core demand, alternative, or provable advantage is unknown.

## 8. Plain-language mental snapshot

Write the decision in this structure:

> For [best-fit customer], when [choice situation], provide [understandable category] through [credible mechanism] to achieve [core value]. Compared with [main alternative], the provable difference is [evidence-backed advantage].

Then add: mental keyword, why to believe, what is deliberately excluded, the most important falsification condition, and the next validation action.

Treat the sentence as a decision summary, not a substitute for evidence.
