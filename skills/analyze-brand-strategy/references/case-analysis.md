# Brand Case Analysis

Use cases to study mechanisms, not to borrow conclusions.

## Case questions

- What decision was the brand actually making?
- Which market conditions and capabilities made it possible?
- What customer behavior changed, and what evidence supports that claim?
- Which part was product, distribution, experience, pricing, culture, or communication?
- What alternative explanation could produce the same observed outcome?
- Which mechanism transfers to the current business, and which does not?

## Common strategic patterns

- **Value consensus:** make quality, scarcity, ritual, status, or trust legible and repeatable.
- **Channel trust:** let selection, service, guarantee, and experience reduce customer risk.
- **Youth strategy:** redesign product, usage occasion, language, and access—not only visual identity.
- **Portfolio strategy:** assign roles to entry, core, premium, and experimental offers.
- **International IP:** balance deep ownership of a few assets with broad licensed distribution; choose based on creative control, capital, speed, and local relevance.

Always re-verify named examples and current figures before client use.
