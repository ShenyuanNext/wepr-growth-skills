# Xiaohongshu Content Execution

## Draft structure

1. **Hook:** state a real tension, mistake, result, or decision.
2. **Context:** define who the method applies to and its boundary.
3. **Cognitive lift:** explain why the obvious approach fails or how the mechanism works.
4. **Execution:** provide ordered actions, thresholds, examples, and checks.
5. **Takeaway:** compress the method into a save-worthy checklist.
6. **Natural hook:** invite a specific contextual question without claiming guaranteed results.

Use short sentences and paragraphs. Add visible anchors such as `01`, `先看`, `判断标准`, `避坑`, or `可直接照做`. Avoid excessive emoji and decorative punctuation.

## Title generation

Generate distinct angles rather than synonyms:

- audience + problem;
- scene + outcome;
- costly mistake + correction;
- counter-intuitive judgment + reason;
- checklist or number + execution object;
- comparison + selection rule;
- experiment or case + method.

Reject titles when they overpromise, hide the subject, depend on fake suspense, use unsupported superlatives, or promise content the body does not deliver. When required, count Chinese characters and keep the title within 20 characters.

## Final check

- Can the reader identify the applicable scenario in five seconds?
- Does every section add a new decision, action, example, or boundary?
- Can the reader execute without contacting the author?
- Is the consultation path optional and natural?
- Are claims, numbers, platform rules, and examples verifiable?
