# Xiaohongshu Account Operations

## Launch foundation

Define the account promise, target reader, proof, three to five content pillars, profile copy, pinned-content plan, and permitted next action before setting a calendar. Build initial material from customer questions, search language, work processes, cases, objections, experiments, and product use. Treat preparation counts and publishing cadence as capacity decisions, not platform requirements.

For an early sample, keep the audience and promise stable while testing one or two variables at a time. Review eligibility and exposure, open, reading depth, saves, comments by motive, profile visits, follows, qualified conversations, and business outcomes. Use the account's own baseline and comparable formats.

## Weekly four-post mix

Use a balanced operating mix rather than four versions of one topic:

1. Search-demand answer: capture an explicit problem.
2. Execution method: demonstrate how work gets done.
3. Case or experiment: show evidence, method, and limits.
4. Decision or checklist: help readers compare, diagnose, or act.

Map every post to an account pillar, reader stage, primary keyword, proof requirement, target action, and reusable derivative.

## Diagnosis sequence

Check in this order:

1. Distribution eligibility and policy risk;
2. Topic–audience fit;
3. Title and cover promise;
4. Opening confirmation;
5. Value density and reading completion;
6. Saves, comments, profile visits, and qualified conversations;
7. Conversion path and response quality.

Do not attribute weak results to “限流” until content, audience, packaging, account health, and measurement alternatives have been checked.

## Natural consultation paths

Good consultation hooks name the missing context: industry, account stage, sample size, objective, or constraint. Avoid urgency, guaranteed outcomes, coded contact instructions, or claims that the post intentionally withheld essential steps.
