# Editorial Illustration Shot List

## Scene selection

Illustrate moments that make an idea easier to understand:

- a person struggling with or operating a system;
- two alternatives with a visible trade-off;
- a sequence, loop, funnel, or bottleneck;
- an abstract force represented as a physical object;
- a consequence that changes the reader's decision.

Skip paragraphs that are already concrete, repetitive, transitional, or dependent on long text.

## Shot-list fields

For each image record:

1. Article placement;
2. Communication job;
3. One-sentence scene;
4. Main subject and action;
5. Dominant composition;
6. Required labels or data;
7. Accent color and emphasis;
8. Aspect ratio and safe margins;
9. Continuity notes;
10. Exclusions.

Default to four to eight images for a substantial article, one to three for a short piece, and fewer when visuals do not add meaning.
