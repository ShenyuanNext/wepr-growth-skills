# Design Xiaohongshu Conversion Paths

Reduce the next decision's uncertainty. Do not treat conversion as pushing every reader into a private message.

## Inputs

Use the account role, target reader, offer, price and decision complexity, delivery model, proof, suitability and exclusions, common objections, current content/profile/pinned posts, current next step, sales or service capacity, and compliance constraints. Do not invent missing product capabilities or results.

## Path model

Map the reader through:

1. **Discovery:** the content matches a real situation, question, or decision.
2. **Qualification:** the post makes fit and non-fit visible.
3. **Trust:** method, proof, process, limits, delivery, and aftercare reduce uncertainty.
4. **Profile acceptance:** the account identity and pinned posts confirm continuity.
5. **Action:** a permitted next step is clear and proportionate to the reader's readiness.
6. **Conversation:** questions qualify context before advice, quotation, or sale.
7. **Delivery:** the operating team can fulfill the promise.
8. **Return:** follow-up content, service, feedback, and referral support longer-term value.

## Offer adaptation

- **Free tool or resource:** optimize for trial, usefulness, feedback, saves, and sharing—not artificial purchase language.
- **Low-consideration product:** answer suitability, use, comparison, price, delivery, return, and support questions.
- **High-consideration service:** demonstrate diagnosis, process, boundaries, cases, fit, timeline, deliverables, and qualification.
- **Brand or editorial account:** use follow, series, search, and trust actions when immediate commercial conversion is inappropriate.

## Workflow

1. Draw the current path and identify the earliest drop-off.
2. List the reader's decision questions at each stage.
3. Assign content jobs: attract, search, trust, proof, qualification, objection, and action.
4. Design profile and pinned-post acceptance.
5. Define public comment actions and private-message qualification questions.
6. Separate useful public information from context-specific advice. Do not withhold essentials merely to force a message.
7. Define permitted action wording and the operating owner for follow-up.
8. Set stage metrics: qualified discovery, useful engagement, profile visit, relevant inquiry, qualification, proposal/trial, transaction, fulfillment, and retention. Use the account's baseline rather than universal benchmarks.
9. Document failure conditions and repair actions.

## Output

Return:

1. current path and earliest friction;
2. target reader, fit, non-fit, and decision obstacles;
3. stage-by-stage content and proof map;
4. profile and three pinned-post jobs;
5. public comment actions and three to five qualification questions;
6. natural action wording for different readiness levels;
7. owner, response process, service-level assumption, and handoff;
8. measurement funnel, review cadence, and repair tests;
9. compliance and delivery risks.

## Guardrails

Do not promise inquiries, conversion rate, sales, or permanent platform access. Do not use fake ordinary users, coordinated comments, hidden advertising, coded external-contact instructions, false scarcity, fabricated proof, or moderation evasion. Verify current official rules before recommending external links, private-message automation, commerce features, advertising disclosure, or sensitive-category actions.
